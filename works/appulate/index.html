Appulate

US InsureTech which help insurance people quote and bind business insurance policies.
I have redesigned the agent's workflow, now it consists of three steps:
  1. Create submission
  2. Request quote
  3. Bind policy
The former workflow was complicated, agents often got lost, and couldn't find information and figure out what to do next. The new one has become more linear and step-by-step.

Main Page

Previously, the agent's main page had a list of insured with submissions were nested in them. Because of this hierarchy, it was difficult to quickly find the right submission.
After conducting research with analysts, we found out that usually the insured have one submission. Therefore, I took out a submission on the main page and made the insured name their parameter.

Creating a New Submission

Due to the changes on the main page, the process of creating a submission has been simplified. Previously, the creation of insurance started automatically after adding a client. The agents did not understand how to create another submission from the same insured and where to look for it after creation.
Now the insurance is created in one step. The client's page is no longer there, and the data from it is displayed on the submission page.
My team and I hope that now agents will find insurance faster and the failure rate will decrease.

Request Quote

After the insurance is created, the agent requests a quote from insurance companies. A quote is a document with the terms and premium of insurance, if they are suitable for the agent's client, the binding procedure will start. Previously, the agent first chose the markets. Their questions were automatically merged into a single form. After filling it out, the agent requested quotes from several markets at once. This approach had problems:
  • The forms got gigantic, with an inverse dependence of questions, a complex structure and nesting.
  • It took a lot of time to develop the algorithm for merging forms of several markets
After observing the work of the agents, we concluded that it was better to make the agent request quotes from the markets one at a time. Advantages of the new approach:
  • Agents get quotes faster as the forms have become shorter.
  • The design of the forms has become simpler, and the structure is controlled manually. This allows us to properly organize the sequence of questions, make a convenient structure and get rid of nesting.
  • Developers have started to add new forms of markets faster.
